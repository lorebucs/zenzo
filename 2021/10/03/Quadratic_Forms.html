from sympy import *
x, y, z = symbols('x y z')
a, b, c, d, e, f = symbols('a b c d e f')

q(x,y) 

x_2d = Matrix([x, y])
A_2x2 = Matrix([[a, b], [b, c]])

x_2d.T * A_2x2 * x_2d
$\displaystyle \left[\begin{matrix}x \left(a x + b y\right) + y \left(b x + c y\right)\end{matrix}\right]$

q(x,y,z) 

x_3d = Matrix([x, y, z])
A_3x3 = Matrix([[a, b, c], [b, d, e], [c, e, f]])

x_3d.T * A_3x3 * x_3d
$\displaystyle \left[\begin{matrix}x \left(a x + b y + c z\right) + y \left(b x + d y + e z\right) + z \left(c x + e y + f z\right)\end{matrix}\right]$

In the general case of an arbitrary number of variables $n$, the quadratic form can still be written using the same compact notation.


\begin{equation} \begin{split} q(x_1,\dots,x_n) &= &\, x_{1} (a_{11} {x_1} + \, &\dots + a_{1n} {x_n}) \, + \\ & &\, x_{2} (a_{21} {x_1} + \, &\dots + a_{2n} {x_n}) \, + \\ & & &\,\, \vdots \\ & &\, x_{n} (a_{n1} {x_1} + \, &\dots + a_{nn} {x_n}) \\ &= &\, \boldsymbol{x}^\intercal A \boldsymbol{x} & \end{split} \end{equation}


Where

\begin{equation} A = \begin{split} \begin{bmatrix} a_{11} & \dots & a_{1n} \\ \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ a_{n1} & \dots & a_{nn} \end{bmatrix} \end{split} \end{equation}


and


\begin{equation} \boldsymbol{x} = \begin{split} \begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ \vdots \\ x_n \end{bmatrix} \end{split} \end{equation}
